Welcome to Kerala

Kerala is a state on the Malabar Coast of India. It was formed on 1 November 1956, following the passage of the States Reorganisation Act, by combining Malayalam-speaking regions of the erstwhile regions of Cochin, Malabar, South Canara, and Travancore. Spread over 38,863 km2 (15,005 sq mi), Kerala is the 21st largest Indian state by area. It is bordered by Karnataka to the north and northeast, Tamil Nadu to the east and south, and the Lakshadweep Sea to the west. With 33 million inhabitants as per the 2011 census, Kerala is the 13th-largest Indian state by population. It is divided into 14 districts with the capital being Thiruvananthapuram. Malayalam is the most widely spoken language and is also the official language of the state.

The Chera dynasty was the first prominent kingdom based in Kerala. Nestled in the enchanting embrace of southwestern India, Kerala emerges as a multifaceted tapestry where nature's splendor, cultural vibrancy, and social progress interweave. With an unparalleled topography that ranges from the idyllic backwaters adorned with houseboats to the pristine beaches kissed by the Arabian Sea, and from the mist-kissed peaks of hill stations to the emerald expanses of tea and spice plantations, Kerala's landscape is a masterpiece painted with diversity. As a testament to its unwavering commitment to education and intellect, Kerala proudly showcases one of the highest literacy rates in India, fostering a populace that values knowledge and critical thinking. Yet, its accolades do not halt here; the state's robust healthcare system, along with a pioneering focus on gender equality and social welfare, consistently sets benchmarks in human development. The symphony of Kerala's cultural heritage echoes through captivating art forms like the spellbinding Kathakali dance and the graceful Mohiniyattam, as well as its mouthwatering culinary offerings, heavily seasoned with coconut and an array of spices. Amidst this melange, Kerala's vibrant festivals, including the grandeur of Onam with its sumptuous feasts and spirited games, spotlight the heartwarming unity of its people. Ultimately, Kerala stands not only as a bewitching panorama of nature's allure but also as a testament to the harmonious coexistence of tradition, progress, and an unwavering reverence for human potential.
